Understanding Waste Classification

Proper waste sorting is crucial for effective recycling and environmental protection. Learn how to identify different types of waste and their correct disposal methods.

Types of Waste

Organic Waste

Biodegradable waste that comes from plants or animals. Examples include food scraps, yard waste, and paper products.

Food scraps Garden waste Wood Paper

Recyclable Waste

Materials that can be processed and turned into new products. Includes paper, plastics, glass, and metals.

Plastic bottles Glass containers Aluminum cans Cardboard

Hazardous Waste

Waste that poses substantial threats to public health and the environment. Requires special handling and disposal.

Batteries Electronics Chemicals Medical waste

Landfill Waste

Waste that cannot be recycled or composted and is sent to landfills. Goal should be to minimize this type.

Styrofoam Certain plastics Diapers Broken ceramics

Benefits of Proper Waste Classification

  • Reduces landfill waste and extends landfill life
  • Conserves natural resources and reduces the need for raw materials
  • Reduces greenhouse gas emissions from landfills
  • Saves energy in manufacturing processes compared to using virgin materials
  • Creates jobs in recycling and waste management industries
